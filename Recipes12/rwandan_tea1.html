rwandan tea

Tea was introduced in Rwanda in 1952 and is now one of the country's largest exports. The fertile volcanic soil and temperate climate make for perfect tea.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of milk
  • 1/2 tsp of loose black tea leaves or tea bag
  • 2 tbsp of sugarr
  • 1 inch piece of fresh ginger

directions

  1. Combine milk, tea, and sugar in a small saucepan and bring to a boil.
  2. Wash the piece of ginger and smash it until juices begin to flow.
  3. Add the ginger to the milk-tea
  4. mixture when it begins to boil.
  5. Reduce the heat and let it simmer for 2-3 minutes.
  6. Strain and serve.
